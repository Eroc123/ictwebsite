Where I'm From
a poem by Eric


I am from roasted chestnuts,

from maple leaves and chilling wind.

I am from heavy clothing and short t-shirts,

snowing and steaming.



I am from the peach tree,

watched it grow from a sapling,

yet yielded no fruit.



I am from small steamed buns,

steamed eggs, and dumplings.

From the cheap fast food

to the expansive hotpots.



I’m from sunny summers

and cloudy winters.

I am from the polluted air

(that barely allows you to see a few paces),

and the vibrant clear skies.



I am from the tall skyscrapers,

rumbling metros and honking cars.

From the crowded shops and fast delivery.



I am from weird superstitions;

Using umbrella indoors makes you short?

Money should always be given in figures of eight?



I’m from the early mornings,

singing anthems under flags.

I am from strict teachers,

who seems to have -

an unlimited supply of homework.

And teachers who barely give any.

I’m from the teachers told me to study study study,

and teachers that just let me play.



From memorizing ABCs,

to chinese character,

and also thai alphabets

(that I still can't remember)


Family tree that stretches far on one side,

yet cut off on the other.


Spent half of my life in one place,

and the other in somewhere else.


I guess,

I am from where I am at.
